Aircons off: New power rate hike casts shadow over Boracay

May 15, 2024

By David Dizon, ABS-CBN News

Boracay resort owners may soon ask tourists to shut off their airconditioning units when not using their rooms as new power rates hikes jack up electricity costs on the island paradise. Aklan Electric Cooperative, which covers the whole of Aklan Province including Boracay, announced it will increase power rates by P5.00/kwh based on preliminary data. This is on top of a P1.49/kwh hike by Akelco last month.

“As of last month pa lang, nagrereklamo na ang mga tao sa increases sa electrical cost, ‘yung billing, e mas lalo na ngayon hinihintay namin yung [May]. Paano ‘yung mga hotels, mga restaurants na dependent sa electricity. So kasali lahat ito, domino reaction,” Nenette Graf, a member of the Boracay Foundation board of trustees, said in a TeleRadyo Serbisyo interview. Graf said resort owners cannot just increase room rates as it will turn off tourists while increasing food prices would also lead to lower sales.


One possible energy conservation measure, she said, is to urge tourists to turn off their AC units when the room is unoccupied.

“Merong mga guests na ayaw magpatay ng aircon, ‘yung di malalaking resorts they tend to leave their rooms na naka on ‘yung aircon tapas ‘yung mga tao bukas ‘yung pinto kahit offices dito, ‘yung local offices,” she said.

Graf backed use of renewable energy sources such as solar and wind while calling for changes in the Wholesale Electricity Spot Market (WESM).
